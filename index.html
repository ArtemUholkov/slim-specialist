Why Choose Tirzepatide or Semaglutide therapy?

GLP-1
Receptor Activation

Tirzepatide and Semaglutide activate GLP-1 receptors, boosting insulin and reducing glucagon, which lowers blood glucose and improves glycemic control.

Insulin Secretion

Activating GLP-1 receptors in pancreatic beta cells stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, helping manage blood sugar levels, especially post-meal.

Glucagon Inhibition

Both medications inhibit glucagon release from pancreatic alpha cells, reducing blood glucose levels by preventing liver glycogen breakdown.

Appetite Suppression
and Weight Loss

By slowing gastric emptying, Tirzepatide and Semaglutide delay nutrient absorption, leading to more stable postprandial glucose levels and improved glycemic control.

Slowing Gastric Emptying

Both medications suppress appetite by acting on brain GLP-1 receptors, aiding significant weight loss, beneficial for obesity management.

Who is eligible to receive a prescription for Semaglutide or Tirzepatide?

Adults who are clinically obese. “Obese” is defined as having a Body Mass Index (BMI) greater than or equal to 30.
Adults who are clinically overweight and have at least one weight-related comorbid condition (e.g. hypertension, high cholesterol, sleep apnea, Arthritis, GERD/Acid reflux). “Overweight” is defined as halving a Body Mass Index (BMI) greater than, or equal to, 27.
There are several BMI calculators online.

I want to use Semaglutide for weight loss, what exactly do I purchase?

First, you will purchase the Semaglutide 2.5mg Starter. This will last you 7 weeks (week 1 through 7).

By week 6, you will then purchase the Semaglutide 5.0mg Standard. This will last you 6 weeks (week 8 through 13).

By week 12, you will then purchase the Semaglutide 10.0mg Premium. This will last you 6 weeks (week 14 through 19).

If you are able to tolerate the maximum dose of Semaglutide 2.0mg once weekly, you will continue to purchase the Semaglutide 10.0mg Premium for the duration of your weight loss journey.

Some patients prefer their maintenance dose to be 1.0mg weekly, or 1.5 mg weekly. They choose not to titrate higher to the maximum dose of 2.0mg weekly. With a lower dose, they experience less gastrointestinal side effects. Studies also show that these lower doses (1.0mg weekly and 1.5mg weekly) lead to weight loss when compared to a placebo group.

I will be using Semaglutide, what is the dosing schedule?

For Semaglutide:
Inject 0.25mg subcutaneously once weekly on weeks 1 to 4.
Inject 0.5mg subcutaneously once weekly on weeks 5 to 9.
Inject 1.0mg subcutaneously once weekly on weeks 10 to 13.
Inject 1.5mg subcutaneously once weekly on weeks 14 to 17.
Then inject 2.0mg subcutaneously once weekly every week thereafter.
